Why the Earth is only planet to have water in all its natural forms?

(Source: ESA/Hubble)
Index > Why the Earth is only planet to have water in all its natural forms?

Why Earth?

In previous article, we discussed, how the water originated on Earth. Then why didn't water originated on other planets, the way it originated on Earth? Why didn't the Asteroids that impacted Earth, also impacted other planets in our solar system?

Actually, the Asteroids did impact other planets in our solar system..!! In the early phases of creation of the solar system, those Asteroids impacted almost every planet in our solar system.

Our Neighbor Planets..

As you may know, Scientists have found water on Mars. But it is very different than Earth's water. Mars does not have oceans and rivers, instead it has water trapped inside rocks in frozen form. This tells us, that water is present on Mars, but only in frozen form. It does not have liquid or gas forms of water.

Unlike Mars, Planet Venus is much hotter. It has methane gas in it's atmosphere, which creates a greenhouse effect, making Venus hottest planet in the solar system. Even hotter than Mercury, which is closest to sun. Therefore, there is no water on Venus, but it has materials necessary for creating life. And it's size is almost the same as of Earth. Venus could harbor life, if the temperature and atmosphere was right on it.

Water was present on those planets also, but only for a short period of time. Water couldn't survive there because these planets are not in Goldilocks Zone..!!


Goldilocks Zone..

Our Earth lies in the Goldilocks Zone. In every solar system, there is a habitable zone, known as Goldilocks Zone. In Goldilocks Zone, the temperature is neither to hot nor too cold. Meaning, the temperature is sustainable for life.


Source: Google

As you can see, Mars and Venus are in the outskirts of this zone. Therefor those are non-habitable planets (yet).


Other Factors..

We know that water exist in three natural forms on earth as ice, water and vapor. In fact, 71% of the Earth's surface is covered with water and the earth is the only planet on which water can exist in liquid form on the surface.

The secret to this coincidence lies both in the Earth's size and its position in the solar system. The earth is big enough so that its atmosphere exerts enough gravitational pull to hold in water vapor (Otherwise water vapour could have leaked into the space..! This phenomenon is known as Atmospheric Escape).

At the same time, it is located 125 million kilometers from the sun. This keeps it near the center of narrow habitable zone, where the temperature allows water to exist in its three natural forms, ice, water and vapor. In the case of our neighboring planets Mars is now a freezed desert and the surface temperature of Venus over 999 degrees F is too much high for ice or liquid water to exist there.

So don't you think that we are lucky to live on this beautiful Earth?
